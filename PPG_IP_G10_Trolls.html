    • 12 senators want probe into alleged government-funded troll farms


    Written by Nadine Roque, Charm Santos-Ocampo, Megan Sison



    When it comes to the Philippine Democratic System, the citizens are the ones who are meant to be in power. The people give this power to the judicial servants so that they can maintain responsibility, in other words the government is simply there to manage and execute the wishes of the people. In order to effectively do this, the government is required to exercise complete transparency in order for the people to properly decide how the government should be run by their elected officials. Unfortunately, recent news of the government attempting to influence the people’s opinions and views by spreading misinformation and distrust through so-called “trolls” have come out. This can lead to serious repercussions in the future, with the representatives elected into office possibly serving only their own interests, rather than that of the people’s. This effectively makes the purpose of a democratic form of government obsolete, as the government is instead acting on their own interests.


    The presence of a multi-party system as well as the ability for different political ideologies to exist are some of the key features of democracy because it embraces how each citizen can display different perspectives on complex and unique situations. However as of late, the different government parties have allegedly funded troll farms which threatens the “free and fair elections” that democracy stands for. These involve fraudulent dealings committed by officials who were elected into power by people who fall for the falsities. These lies were then spewed by the trolls that were hired to make these official’s motivations for a seat in the government far more appealing to the masses. In a political situation, troll farms could be used to fabricate or ruin one’s reputation which, according to this case, happened to some of President Duterte’s critics. The government failed to be transparent and just to its people, as they served not their own country but their own selfish motivations. In a more idealistic situation, democratic citizens are meant to treat each other with respect and equality since everyone is supposed to be granted the same status and rights as none are above the law, including the officials serving the government.


    Every single vote counts, and it is timely to consider when the 2022 election of the Philippines is coming in a couple of months. We need a competent leader to serve our country without a hidden agenda clouding their spirit of service and nationalism. This should be the case, but how will we be able to discern what is true or false if the proliferation of misinformation has spread without our knowledge? If these ‘public relations practitioners’ and ‘social media consultants’ are being given malicious funds behind taxpayers’ backs, the citizen’s trust on the government will be violated. A politician’s image founded on lies is a dangerous tool when it could malign a voter’s reality. To uphold a proper democracy, it is the citizen’s job to discredit clickbait, bias and unreliable sources. We should elect candidates that are transparent with funding the right programs, candidates who fight for the right principles and candidates who maintain the integrity of what a democracy truly is.
