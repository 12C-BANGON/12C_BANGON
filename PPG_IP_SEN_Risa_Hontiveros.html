    • Ana Theresia "Risa" Hontiveros

    Running for Senator, Akbayan


    Researched by Faith Villaluz



    Education


    1. St. Scholastica's College Manila - Primary Education (1979)


    2. St. Scholastica's College Manila - Secondary School (1983)


    3. Bachelor of Arts in Social Sciences (1987)

    - Ateneo de Manila University

    - Cum Laude


    4. Managing Arts Program (2004)

    - Diploma Course, Asian Institute of Management





    Accomplishments


    1. She received the Ten Outstanding Young Men (TOYM) Award for Peace Advocacy for her work in the peace talks with the national Democratic Front (NDF) in 2001.


    2. Senator Risa was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize in 2005, along with 27 other Filipinas.


    3. She served as a member of the Board of Directors of the Philippine Health Insurance Corporation (PhilHealth) from 2014-2015. She pushed for expanded benefits and coverage, specifically for indigents and senior citizens, while in this position.


    4. Prior to her position in Senate, Risa Hontiveros worked as a two-term partylist congressional representative. Together with civil society groups and other advocates, she worked for the passage of the Reproductive Health (RH) Law. She was also one of the principal authors of the Cheaper Medicines Law.


    5. In Sen. Risa's first term as senator, she passed 13 laws:

    - The Expanded Maternity Leave Law

    - The strengthened Anti-Hospital Deposit Law

    - The Special Protection of Children in Situations of Armed Conflict Law

    - The Bangsamoro Organic Law

    - The Universal Healthcare Law

    - The Philippine HIV & AIDS Policy Law

    - The amended Magna Carta for Persons with Disabilities

    - The Philippine Mental Health Law, the Philippines’ first true national policy on mental health

    - The Safe Streets and Public Spaces Law

    - The First 1000 Days Law, also known as the Kalusugan at Nutrisyon ng mga Nanay Law

    - The Speech Pathology Law

    - The Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program Law

    - The Simulated Birth Rectification Law


    6. Senator Hontiveros recently re-filed the SOGIE Equality Bill and the Divorce Bill as part of her flagship measures for the new Congress. She also re-filed a measure to further strengthen the Anti-Hospital Detention Law.


    7. Additional filed measures by Risa Hontiveros include:

    - The Universal Social Pension for Senior Citizens Bill

    - The Girls not Brides Bill

    - The Prevention of Teenage Pregnancy Bill

    - The Expanded Solo Parents Welfare Bill

    - The On-Site, In-City, Near City Resettlement Program Bill

    - A bill for the creation of a Department of Ocean, Fisheries, and Aquatic Resources

    - The Magna Carta for Seafarers

    - TThe Bibong Health Workers Bill

    - The Students Rights and Welfare Bill

    - The National Land Use Management Act

    - The Anti-Elder Abuse Act

    - The Public Health intervention for Drug Use Act





    Advocacies


    1. Risa Hontiveros’ advocacies revolve around health and women’s rights. She currently chairs the Senate Committee on Women, Family Relations, and Gender Equality.


    2. In 2021, Senator Hontiveros launched the “Healthy Pinas Mobile Clinic” to provide free medical services to Filipinos residing in poor, far-flung areas in the Philippines.


    3. She filed a resolution in 2021, declaring July 12 of every year as the “National West Philippine Sea Victory Day” to commemorate the Philippines’ triumph before the Court of Arbitration (PCA). She also added that it was meant to honor the efforts of late President “Noynoy” Aquino III in “securing a landmark legal victory upholding the country’s sovereign rights over the West Philippine Sea.”





    Corruption Cases


    Pharmally Pharmaceutical Corporation filed a sedition complaint against Senator Risa Hontiveros in 2021 for allegedly bribing a witness to appear in a Senate Probe. However, Senator Hontiveros claims to have evidence to disprove these claims and calls the whole situation a “last-ditch effort” by the company to derail the ongoing Senate probe into their alleged procurement deal with the Philippine government.

    Family Background


    1. Risa Hontiveros married Francisco Baraquel Jr. in 1990, but was widowed in 2005.


    2. She has four children: Kiko Baraquel, Ianna Baraquel, Sinta Baraquel, and Issa Baraquel.


    3. Her sister, Pia Hontiveros, is a Broadcaster at CNN Philippines.


    4. She is related to actor Luis Hontiveros.
