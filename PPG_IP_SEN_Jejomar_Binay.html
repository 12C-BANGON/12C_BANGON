    5. Mayor of the Municipality of Makati

    - After Mayor Nemesio I. Yabut died during the EDSA Revolution, President Corazon Aquino appointed him as one of the first local executives. He was elected on January 18, 1988, and re-elected on May 11, 1992, and May 8, 1995.

    - His active defense of the Constitution earned him the nickname "Rambotito" (after the movie hero Rambo), the Outstanding Achievement Medal, and Aquino's recognition.

    - Binay become mayor again and won over then-vice mayor Edu Manzano in a landslide on May 14, 2001. On May 10, 2004, he won over 1st District Councilor Oscar Ibay. On May 14, 2007, he ran for his sixth and final term as mayor, beating out actor and Senator Lito Lapid. His victory over Lapid was the largest margin in a Makati City election.


    6. Vice President of the Republic of the Philippines

    - He ran as Jospeh Estrada's running mate for PDP-Laban (Partido Demokratiko Pilipino-Lakas ng Bayan). He was elected as Vice President in May 2010.

    - President Noynoy Aquino named him chairman of the Housing Urban Development Coordinating Council (HUDCC). He also headed the Task Force OFW, which helps abused overseas Filipino workers return home with the help of the government.

    Advocacies


    1. On March 20, 2015, Vice President Binay announced his 2016 Presidential platform at the Integrated Bar of the Philippines in Cebu City. He wants to improve conditions in public colleges and universities, hospitals, police stations, and mass housing. Salary and benefit increases for teachers, nurses, police officers, and other public servants were also promised. His plans include redesigning and reengineering the country's transportation system, prioritizing infrastructure and job creation. He proposes amending the constitution to stimulate the economy and accelerate the country's growth.

    Corruption Cases


    1. In 2016, the Ombudsman filed corruption charges against former Vice President Jejomar Binay at the Sandiganbayan for allegedly overcharging for the Makati Parking Building. He was charged with violating Section 3A of the Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act, which defines an official as one who induces another public official to violate certain rules and regulations.


    2. Jejomar “Jojo” Binay was administratively liable for grave misconduct, dishonesty, gross inexcusable negligence, graft, and corrupt practices involving the allegedly overpriced Makati City Hall Parking Building II. The construction of five phases of the Makati City Hall Parking Building II, costing a total of P2.28 billion, revealed "inconsistencies in budgeting, procurement, and execution that resulted in the misappropriation of public funds." The P11.97 million contract for architectural and engineering services with contractor MANA Architecture and Interior Design Co (MANA) is not covered by an appropriation and was awarded through "simulated bidding and payments" that were not accompanied by the required documents and information.


    3. Jejomar “Jojo” Binay and his son, former Makati City Mayor Jejomar Erwin "Junjun" Binay, were charged Friday with graft and falsification in connection with the P1.3 billion Makati Science High School building procurement. Prosecutors from the Ombudsman filed four counts of violating the Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act and three counts of falsification in the Sandiganbayan against the Binays. Makati City officials allegedly forged documents to give the impression that there were additional bidders for the separate engineering and construction contracts awarded to Infiniti Architectural Works and Hilmarc's Construction Corp.





    Scandals


    1. Binay was suspended for 60 days in 2006, along with then-vice mayor Ernesto Mercado and all city councilors, for allegedly padding city hall's payroll with "ghost" employees. The suspension was halted by a Court of Appeals temporary restraining order, but not before Cory Aquino and other Binay supporters marched to city hall to show their support for the former Makati mayor. Malacanang interpreted the suspension as political retaliation against Binay for his role in protests against then-President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo.


    2. In the year 2007, the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) froze the city government's bank accounts as well as those of Binay and Mercado, alleging that the city owed the BIR P1.1 billion in withholding taxes on city employees between 1999 and 2002. Additionally, the BIR had issued a garnishment warrant against the aforementioned bank accounts.


    3. Binay's alleged extramarital affair was also exposed online in 2010. He has admitted to having an extramarital affair that came dangerously close to destabilizing his marriage to Dr. Elenita Binay.





    Family Background


    1. He is the younger of Diego "Jego" Medrano Binay's two children. His father is a librarian from Bauan, Batangas, and his mother is a school teacher from Cabagan, Isabela. He had an older brother who died prior to his birth, making him the only remaining family member.


    2. After being orphaned at a young age, Binay went on to live with his uncle Ponciano Binay. They resided in the former red light district area known as Kuli-Kuli in Barangay Pio del Pilar, Makati.


    3. His wife, Elenita "Ellen" Sombillo Binay, M.D. is a Filipino politician and physician. She served as mayor of Makati from 1998 to 2001.


    4. Binay and his wife have 5 children together, who are the following: Ma. Lourdes Nancy B. Angeles, Mar-Len Abigail B. Campos, Jejomar Erwin S. Binay, Jr., Marita Angeline B. Alcantara, and Joanna Marie Bianca S. Binay.


    5. Some of his children are also involved in politics, namely Ma. Lourdes Nancy B. Angeles, a senator, Mar-Len Abigail B. Campos, an Elected Representative of The Second District of Makati, and Jejomar Erwin S. Binay, former Mayor of the City of Makati.
