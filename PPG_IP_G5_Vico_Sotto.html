    • Analysis on "Vico Sotto named ‘anti-corruption champion’ by US state department"


    Written by Rafi Hernandez, Moira Ibarrola, Ariana Jimenez



    Iloilo is a good example of exemplary governance, given its current state. The city shows the effects of a properly executed democratic system, and the potential of the country if the system were effective nation-wide. This is why Iloilo is an excellent case of a good democratic system. For example, the economy of Iloilo is in a superior state as compared to the rest of the nation’s cities, especially during the pandemic. This is because of the tourism in Iloilo, and the enhanced urbanization of the city. This is a good example of how and why private sectors or businesses heavily affect the government. It demonstrates how businesses that help develop a city through urbanization can boost the economy of a city through tourism (and even domestically), and on a macro level, a country. This is relevant to democracy because in this kind of government, private sectors are considered by the government when making decisions, given the fact that they greatly influence the economy. Iloilo is a good example as to why this is the case.

    The Philippine democratic system is defined by the supreme power and authority from all Filipinos, and its duty is to respect and work for the interests of the citizens of the country. This system brings power to the national government, which is decentralized to the local government units such as in municipalities and cities. One of the essential characteristics of the Philippine Democratic government is Liberalism. Liberalism advocates the securing of freedom of citizens from any force that seeks to limit it. It is the firm belief in which the individual has the capacity to develop a democratic space for a citizen to focus on their own development. Another essential characteristic of the Philippine Democratic government is the presence of Local Government Units (LGU) which serves as a system to create appropriate and relevant solutions to local problems. It holds the power to enact ordinances if necessary, and is connected to the national government. These are divided into provinces, cities, municipalities, and barangays.


    Pasig City is only one of the many cities in the Philippines. The Pasig City Council is an example of a legislative body of a city in which Vico Sotto ran for a seat in 2016, and won. He is now the mayor of Pasig city, and has recently been awarded the name of an ‘Anti-Corruption Champion’ by the US State Department along with 12 other international government officials. This title serves as a recognition of a person’s “leadership, courage, and impact in preventing, exposing, and combating corruption.” (Gonzales, 2021). Sotto was recognized internationally as a “standard-bearer” for future Philippine public servants who see the urgency and necessity of anti corruption and transparency initiatives. This issue is prominent in our Philippine democratic system, and Sotto has exhibited his genuine concern for it, which is uncommonly heard from civil servants.


    Sotto’s recent acquisition of this name has proven that he is a dignified leader under the Philippine democratic system. The democratic government is “for the people”, meaning that its purpose is to serve the needs of the people. Sotto implemented that by consistently fighting against corruption. According to the Merriam-Webster Thesaurus, corruption is “dishonest and unlawful behavior especially by people of power such as government officials or police officers” (Merriam Webster, 2021). Through Sotto’s efforts to protect the people and their rights, he “defends transparency and ensures accountability in the country” (Gonzales, 2021). He was able to promote and acclaim the philosophy of liberalism, which is an important feature of the Philippine democratic system. Moreover, as mentioned previously, the Philippine government is also connected to the Local Government Units (LGU). Vico Sotto was able to use this for the betterment of his city. He was able to create a law during his time in the city council that allowed the freedom of citizens to access information held by government institutions, otherwise known as information legislation. Additionally, he made it a point to enable civil society organisations’ involvement in Pasig city’s budgeting and policy making (Gonzales, 2021). These are among the several works that Sotto did as a public servant. Not only has he proven that he serves according to the needs of the people, but he also ensures that all citizens are heard and valued.


    In summary, Vico Sotto being named an Anti-Corruption Champion has reflected how a government official should practice his power to leverage the development of the country, and to protect the well-being of the masses. He was able to utilize the opportunities given by the way the Philippine Democratic system functions. Sotto serves as a great example for future public officials, and we can only hope that the Philippines is able to elect more politicians such as him.
