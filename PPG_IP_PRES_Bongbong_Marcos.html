    2. Congressman of Second District, Ilocos Norte (1992 - 1995; 2007 - 2010)

    - HB No. 2212 / RA No. 7638: Department of Energy Act of 1992

    - HB No. 11614 / RA No. 8044: Youth in Nation-Building Act

    Corruption Cases


    1. Numerous cases have been filed against the Marcos family for their ill-gotten wealth and human rights violations during the Martial Law period. Confirmed or convicted cases include:

    - Sandiganbayan’s 2014 declaration ruling the Malacanang Jewelry Collection as part of the Marcoses’ ill-gotten wealth

    - Imelda Marcos’ conviction of seven counts of graft for the creation and maintenance of private foundations in Switzerland while in office from 1968 - 1986. It was later found in 2018 that the Marcos children, including Bongbong Marcos, were beneficiaries of these organizations.

    - Around P1 billion hidden stolen wealth that the Sandiganbayan demanded back earlier this September


    2. Most of them however, have been dismissed by the Sandiganbayan or courts found both in and outside of the Philippines. A great number of them also remain pending or have been archived.



    Scandals


    1. Earlier this year, controversy sparked due to an interview Marcos had with television personality Toni Gonzaga-Soriano. Many, including the Ateneo Martial Law Museum, accused him of historical revisionism.


    2. Most recently, two petitions have been filed against Marcos, seeking to disqualify him as a presidential candidate. They both were made due to claims that he failed to file income tax returns and pay income taxes.
