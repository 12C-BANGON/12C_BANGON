    • Analysis on "Register for 2022 polls, voters urged"


    Written by Sarah Deakin, Carmela Dioko, Camille Duran



    Maricel Cruz’s article entitled “Register for 2022 polls, voters urged” published on manilastandard.net on February 1, 2021 informs readers on Rizal Representative Fidel Nograles’ statements on the urgency and importance of registering for the national elections coming up next year. It also includes some of his suggestions to increase the number of registered voters, as well as information from the Commission of Elections (COMELEC) on the current state of, and the commission’s initiatives for, the ongoing voter’s registration. This article applied the concepts of democratic government systems, citizen participation, and the vital role of the Local Government Units (LGUs).


    Democracy is commonly defined as a government of, for, and by the people. This definition of democracy is discussed throughout the article, as Cruz reminds us of the importance of voting. The article opens with, "voters are the ultimate arbiters on how a country should be run" (Cruz, 2021); this statement shows that the government is of and by the people. We, the citizens, are given the power to create our government that we know will help us fulfill the country's needs by freely and fairly voting for any running officer. She carries on to say that the "elections are an opportunity to implement change," and the "youth should not pass up the chance to elect those they think should lead" (Cruz, 2021). These statements remind us that the government is for the people, as we have the advantage to vote for those whom we know will serve the country correctly. Cruz applies concepts of democracy here by reminding us how each of our votes is important to create good change for our country.


    Secondly, the article directly relates to the key concept of Citizenship and Citizen Participation. It reported on the urging by Rizal Rep. Nograles to Filipino citizens to register for the 2022 National Elections. He said that “voters are the ultimate arbiters on how the country should be run” (Cruz, 2021) and this is definitely true and an extremely relevant message to convey right now. As the Philippines is a democratic nation, the Filipino citizens must practice participation, civility and patience in order to determine or at least aid in the success of the government. The first and foremost way to do this is to exercise the right as well as carry out the responsibility to vote and pick the leaders. As discussed in the lecture video, suffrage is the first direct exercise of citizen power and the only regular exercise of sovereignty in a republican democracy. This article is spreading awareness on this crucial civic duty of Filipinos as the period for voters’ registration is coming to an end in September 2021.


    Lastly, the article stated that despite the difficulties regarding the current COVID-19 protocols, voting has become much more accessible for citizens to accomplish. With the help of Local Government Units, Rizal Rep. Fidel Nograles has proposed to resolve the issues of public transport by looking into the possibility of local governments and the private sector providing transportation for citizens who plan on registering to vote but do not have the means to leave their homes. Aside from just providing public transportation, barangay registration satellites have also been suggested by these officials. Nograles said, "barangay registration satellites could be a good solution for people who are still averse to going out, especially as public transportation still remains an issue." This means that Local Government Units (LGUs) have provided links between the government and the people by allowing citizens to fulfill their democratic rights by voting in an obtainable and convenient manner. By providing residents with the means to vote, the needs of all sectors have the opportunity to be assisted.


    Overall, the article exemplifies the three concepts of democracy, citizen participation, and the Local Government Units’ (LGUs) role in voting registration. The audience is reminded of the definition and importance of democracy. Cruz states how registering to vote will help the citizens create a government that will change the country's needs. Additionally, the importance of citizenship participation is emphasized, as she mentions the power Filipino citizens have to choose their government through free and fair elections. Lastly, the article also mentions citizens not having the means to vote and shared how the Local Government Units (LGUs) planned on helping them. In conclusion, the article reminds its audience on the importance of registering to vote because the future and change needed by our country depends on its citizens.
