    Advocacies


    1. Human Rights Defender (Justice and extra-judicial killings): Chairperson of the Commission on Human Rights under the Arroyo administration; She had high-profile cases such as the Davao death squads, human rights cases against Jovito Palparan, and the Maguindanao massacre in 2009. She was the face of the anti-EJK campaign in the Philippines.


    2. Social Inequality: She supported the Universal Access to Quality Tertiary Education Act in the Senate, despite not being able to vote for it as she was imprisoned at the time. She also supported the LGBT-backed SOGIE Equality Bill. In June 2020, she called on the Philippine Senate to investigate alleged reports that the Philippine National Police was "red-tagging" progressive groups through its official social media accounts.


    3. Climate Change and Disasters: She believes that the PH-US Enhanced Defense Cooperation Agreement (EDCA) will serve the Filipino people well since it can be used to aid the Philippines when disasters strike. She supports the Paris Agreement, especially since the Philippines is one of the most vulnerable countries when it comes to climate change.


    4. Foreign Policy: She supports the partnership of the United States and the Philippines, calling the Supreme Court's favorable ruling on EDCA as a "much needed boost" to the country's armed forces modernization. She was a member of the Visiting Forces Agreement Commission.


    5. Peace in Mindanao: She was one of the few personalities in government who continued to support the Bangsamoro Basic Law as she believed that Muslim Filipinos have the right to be given such legislation. She defended the constitutionality of the Bangsamoro Basic Law against anti-Muslim personalities.





    Corruption Cases


    Allegations of receiving drug protection money:

    - In 2016, President Rodrigo Duterte accused De Lima of having an affair with her driver, Ronnie Dayan, who Duterte also allegedly functioned as De Lima's collector for drug protection money when she was the Justice secretary. Duterte claimed that he had wiretaps and ATM records which confirmed these allegations. In September of the same year, De Lima was removed from her position chairing a Senate Justice and Human Rights committee investigating extrajudicial killings. She later admitted that she had a relationship with Dayan many years ago. Justice secretary Vitaliano Aguirre called on convicted drug lords, former prison officials and police officers as prime witnesses against De Lima in the Congressional probe on illegal drug trafficking in the New Bilibid Prison. Albuera, Leyte Mayor Rolando Espinosa had corroborated allegations that De Lima benefited from the illegal drug activities of his son Kerwin Espinosa in Eastern Visayas. Included in the Mayor's affidavit is a picture of De Lima with Kerwin Espinosa in Baguio City. Kerwin Espinosa later testified that he gave her a total of P8 milion to help finance her senatorial campaign during elections in May 2016.

    Family Background


    1. She is the eldest daughter of Vicente de Lima and Norma Magistrado.

    - Vicente is the the former Philippine COMELEC Commissioner.


    2. Her aunt, Julie de Lima, is married to Jose Maria Sison, making him Leila de Lima's uncle by marriage.

    - Jose Maria Sison is the Communist Party of the Philippines founder.


    3. She has two children, namely Vincent Joshua Bohol and Israel de Lima.

    - Israel is the eldest son, followed by his half brother Vincent.


    4. She is divorced from lawyer Plaridel Bohol.
