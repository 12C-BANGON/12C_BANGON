    • Jose "Lito" Atienza

    Running for Vice President, Provinces First Development Initiative


    Researched by Cristina Claparols



    Education


    Bachelor of Science in Architecture, University of Santo Tomas





    Accomplishments


    1. Deputy Speaker, House of Representatives (2020 - present)


    2. Buhay party-list representative (2013 - present)

    - House Bill 1108 to reorganize the Department of Education into the Department of Education, Culture, and Sports. This aims to strengthen the sports and cultural training among the youth. (2019)

    - “Pilipino Milyonaryo” Bill, or House Bill 1107, which incentivises the elderly to live until the age of 101 to be able to share a P1 million peso grant.

    - House Bill 3839, to avoid airing foreign series or teleseryes from being shown during prime time (6PM - 10PM). This sought to combat the issue of Filipino-made films being pushed into unfavorable time slots for airing. (2014)

    - House Bill 3840, an amendment to Republic Act 9167, which aims to provide a 5-year tax holiday for the local film industry. This aims to help local filmmakers recover and grow more competitive. (2014)


    3. Secretary, Department of Environment and Natural Resources (2007 - 2009)

    - Ordered the dismantling of illegal fish pens in Laguna de Bay (2008)


    4. Manila Mayor (1998 - 2007)

    - Developed the Buhayin ang Maynila program


    5. Manila Vice Mayor (1992 - 1998)

    - Set up an orphanage called Home for the Angels for abandoned babies (1995)


    6. Manila Assemblyman (1984 - 1986)


    7. Established the Mahal Ko si Lolo, Mahal Ko si Lola foundation





    Advocacies


    1. Wants to abolish ineffective government agencies and support cooperatives that can allow for economic recovery and better pandemic responses. (2021)


    2. Objected to the passing of a bill allowing absolute divorce in the country. (2021)


    3. Managed a program called Buhayin ang Maynila





    Corruption Cases


    In 2011, he was sued for a P4.5-billion plunder case due to the allegedly overpriced dredging project at the Pasig River





    Scandals


    1. In 2015, it was admitted by Manila that a 2000 ordinance that was issued by Atienza violated women’s reproductive health rights. EO 003 showed to discourage the use of artificial methods of contraception, while EO 030 implemented a ban from funding modern contraceptives.


    2. In 2001, started a program to build the City College of Manila on historical botanical gardens. Conservationists who disapproved of the project insisted that construction could destroy the site.


    3. In 2000, Atienza was credited for ordering the demolition of the Manila Jai Alai Building, despite its historical significance that sparked a strong opposition against the destruction.





    Family Background


    1. Married to Ma. Evelina Ilagan


    2. Father of Arnold Atienza and Kim Atienza
